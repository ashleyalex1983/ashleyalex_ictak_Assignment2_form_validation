THRISSUR

Thrissur is also called as cultural capital of Kerala India. The term Thrissur is the abbreviated anglicized form of the Malayalam word "Thrissivaperur" which means the town of the "Sacred Shiva ". The town is built on an elevated ground, at the apex of which is the famous "Vadakkumnathan Temple".A place of great antiquity, Thrissur was also known as "Vrishabhadipuram" in ancient days. The district is famous for its ancient temples, churches, and mosques.

Other interesting sites are the Zoo, Fort, Museum, Church and Temple. The important crops grown are paddy, coconut, tapioca, areca nut, cashew, banana, rubber, pepper, turmeric, pulses etc. Kerala Kalamandalam, Kerala Sahithya Akademi, Kerala Lalithakala Akademi and Kerala Sangeetha Nadaka Akademi are situated in Thrissur district. The largest mosque is situated, Thrissur in India. Peechi Dam, Athirapally- vazhachal are situated in Thrissur district.

Top 5 Destinations



Athirapally and Vazhachal Falls

A shimmering expanse of tranquil beauty, this waterfall of 80 ft. high is 30 km east of Chalakkudy. Athirappally attracts a large number of eco tourists. Vazhachal, another breathtaking waterfall is 5 km from Athirappally. Athirapally Falls is at the height of 80 feet high is turbulent and violent while Vazhachal Fall is calm and cool.

Vadakkumnathan Shiva Temple

This is not just another place for worship. It has been the repository of culture and heritage unparalleled in Kerala history. Vadakku in Malayalam means north, but in this case, it is the corrupted form of the word 'Edvam' or 'Sacred Bull'. 'Kunnu' is the Malayalam word for hill. Thus 'Edvakkunnathan' became 'Vadakkunnathan'. The deity is Lord Siva represented by 'Mahalingam'.

Chimmony Dam

Chimmony Dam is located in Echippara in Thrissur District. The dam is built across Chimoni river and the construction of the dam was completed in 1996 . Dam is surrounded by lush green dense forests and offers beautiful view of greenery of Kerala State. Chimmony Wildlife sanctuary is also located nearby the Dam.

Cheraman Juma Masjid

Kodungallur constructed in A.D. 629 is famous as the first mosque erected in India and also the first in India and second in the world where the Juma prayers were started. People from far and near irrespective of caste and creed visit this holy place and pay their homage. Eid al-Fitr (Ramsan) and Eid ul-Adha (Bakrid) are celebrated here on a grand scale.

Our Lady of Dolours Basilica

Our Lady of Dolours Basilica church is built in fine Indo-Gothic style architecture is located in Thrissur city. It is the third tallest church of Asia spread over an area of 25,000 square feet. The church is also known as Puthan Pally. This church is largest in India and its interiors have beautiful murals, images of saints and scenes from the scriptures.
