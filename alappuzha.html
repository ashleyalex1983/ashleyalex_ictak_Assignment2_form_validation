ALAPPUZHA

Alappuzha is a landmark between the broad Arabian Sea and a net work of rivers flowing into it. In the early first decade of the 20th Century the then Viceroy of the Indian Empire, Lord Curzon made a visit in the State to Alleppey, now Alappuzha. Fascinated by the Scenic beauty of the place, in joy and amazement, he said, Here nature has spent up on the land her richest bounties. In his exhilaration, it is said, he exclaimed, Alleppey, the Venice of the East. Thus the sobriquet found its place in the world Tourism Map.

Interlocked by a large number of canals and bridges, Alappuzha has immense untapped potential for backwater tourism. It has several canals and lakes fit for boat cruise. Three prominent rivers in south Kerala viz. Achankovil, Pamba and Manimala empty into the lakes. A boat journey through the lakes will be a memorable experience. The watercourses are flanked by an unending row of coconut trees.

The economy of the district is based on agriculture and marine products. The smallest district of Kerala is Alappuzha. It is also the most density populated district in Kerala. The famous Nagaraja Temple is at Mannarasala here.

Top 5 Destinations



Alappuzha Beach

Alappuzha Beach also known as Alleppey Beach. This beautiful beach with a silver stretch of sand surrounded by palm grooves is one of the most popular tourist attraction of the district and Kerala. . Alappuzha Lighthouse a 140 years old Lighthouse is located near the beach which makes it a must visit beach.

Krishnapuram Palace Museum

Krishnapuram Palace is a Museum located in Kayamkulam near Allepey in Alappuzha district. The Krishnapuram Palace was built during the reign of Marthanda Varma, a Travancore king. The palace itself was built to showcase the traditional style of architecture native to the land.

Marari Beach

Marari beach is about 11 km from Alleppey town. Alleppey known for its breathtaking backwaters is famous tourist destination of Kerala that attracts hoards of tourist from all over the world. Marari Beach is in the Mararikulam a small fishing village in the outskirts of Alappuzha.

Water Carnival at Punnamada

Nehru Trophy Boat Race, the annual water carnival at Punnamada Lake, is considered the biggest snake boat race in the world. The colourful boat pageantry, which precedes the boat race is a treat to the eyes.

Kuttanad

Backwaters of Kuttanad "Rice Bowl of Kerala". Here farming is done below sea level and is one of the few places in the world where farming is done below sea level. Backwaters of Kuttanad region in R block is memorable one and offers great scenic views.
